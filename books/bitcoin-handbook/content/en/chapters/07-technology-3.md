# Technology 3: Thermodynamics ~ Gall’s Law

## Chapter Digest

This unit binds Bitcoin to physical law. Thermodynamics, the arrow of time, information theory, Moore’s Law, antifragility, and Gall’s Law are all used to show that Bitcoin is not just code floating in abstraction. It is a system whose security depends on real energy expenditure, real computation, and the irreversible ordering of events. Proof-of-work is what translates digital consensus into physical cost.

Bhatia’s broader claim is that Bitcoin survives because it aligns with rather than denies physical constraints. It metabolizes energy into ordered history. That makes the chain expensive to falsify and increasingly difficult to reverse. Gall’s Law adds that complex working systems tend to evolve from simpler working systems, which reinforces why Bitcoin’s gradual accretion matters more than feature overload.

The lesson here is that Bitcoin is a technological system rooted in reality, not merely in software preference. Its durability comes from the union of computation, energy, and time.

## Key Takeaways

- Bitcoin’s security is grounded in physical cost.
- Proof-of-work links computation to thermodynamic reality.
- The arrow of time matters for irreversible settlement history.
- Gall’s Law favors gradual evolution over overloaded design.
- Bitcoin’s durability depends on reality constraints, not their suspension.
