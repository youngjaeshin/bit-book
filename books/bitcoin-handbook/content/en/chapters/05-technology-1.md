# Technology 1: Orders of Magnitude ~ Friction

## Chapter Digest

The first technology unit retrains the reader’s intuition for scale. Orders of magnitude matter because exponential systems cannot be judged with linear instincts. Bitcoin’s security and adoption both depend on numerical realities that appear abstract until translated into relative scale. From there the handbook moves through improvement, network effects, churn, and friction, showing how technological adoption is shaped by both compounding performance and user switching costs.

Bitcoin thrives when its advantages are not merely marginal but order-of-magnitude improvements over incumbent systems. Yet adoption is never frictionless. Network effects give incumbents power, while churn and friction slow migration even toward better tools. This is why Bitcoin’s progress often feels slower than its conceptual superiority would suggest.

The unit’s core lesson is that systems change through compounding edge plus survivable onboarding friction. Scale, improvement, and adoption resistance must be studied together.

## Key Takeaways

- Exponential systems require order-of-magnitude thinking.
- Bitcoin adoption depends on both technical edge and social friction.
- Network effects can slow migration even when a technology is superior.
- Churn and switching costs shape the path of adoption.
- Scale and usability must be read together.
