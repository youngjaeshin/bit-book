# Technology 1: Orders of Magnitude ~ Friction

## Chapter Digest

The discussion begins by resetting intuition about scale. Orders of magnitude matter because exponential change cannot be read with linear habits, and Bitcoin’s growth only makes sense when price, adoption, and network size are viewed on a logarithmic frame. A tenfold gain in adoption does not just add users; it multiplies the possible connections among them, which is why the network effect can become so powerful so quickly.

From there, the 10x improvement rule clarifies why new systems displace old ones only when their advantage is obvious enough to overcome inertia. Bitcoin’s edge is not one feature, but a bundle of improvements: always-on access, final settlement, censorship resistance, auditability, and a bearer asset that can be moved and stored without a custodian. Even with those advantages, adoption still has to work through churn and friction, because people and institutions do not switch monetary systems just because a better one exists.

Friction is the decisive bottleneck. Traditional finance still moves value through industrial-age procedures full of delays, permissions, and intermediary risk, while Bitcoin turns money into information that can move through digital communication networks directly. That shift matters because the protocol is not just another app; it is a set of rules layered onto the internet itself. Once value becomes pure information, it can inherit the same communication advantages that transformed everything else online.
