# Why Bitcoin

## Chapter Digest

The final argument ties the earlier threads together and asks why Bitcoin matters more than the other options available. The answer is that Bitcoin combines fixed issuance, decentralization, financial sovereignty, and censorship resistance in one system. It is not just a technical network. It is a monetary revolution that offers a direct alternative to concentrated financial power.

Central bank digital currencies serve as the sharpest contrast. Where Bitcoin disperses control and places responsibility with the user, CBDCs would make it easier for the state to see, shape, and intervene in monetary life. Bitcoin therefore matters not only for what it enables, but also for what it prevents: arbitrary monetary expansion, political control over balances, and the use of money as a surveillance tool.
