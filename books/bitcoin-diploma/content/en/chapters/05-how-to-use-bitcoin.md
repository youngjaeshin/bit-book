# How to Use Bitcoin

## Chapter Digest

The practical use of Bitcoin is presented as the bridge between understanding and participation. Once Bitcoin is recognized as a monetary alternative, the next question is how an ordinary person acquires it, stores it, sends it, and receives it. Wallets, transactions, and Lightning payments are treated as essential literacy, not optional extras, because they turn theory into a lived form of sovereignty.

Using Bitcoin also changes the shape of dependence. Instead of relying entirely on banks or payment processors, users learn how to manage funds more directly, and layered payment systems such as Lightning extend Bitcoin’s usefulness into everyday exchange. Practical participation is therefore part of monetary education itself.
