# History of Money and Fiat Control

## Chapter Digest

This unit traces the movement from barter to commodity money and then to state-controlled fiat money. The story is not framed as simple progress. It is presented as a series of responses to exchange problems, storage needs, and political pressure. Commodity money appears because some goods are accepted more broadly and preserve value better than others, while fiat money becomes possible once states gain the power to define and monopolize the monetary unit.

It then connects fiat control to institutional crisis and political management. Once money is separated from hard constraints and placed under central authority, inflation, manipulation, and moral hazard become built-in possibilities rather than rare accidents. The modern system can still function, but it does so by giving a small number of institutions unusual power over everyone else’s purchasing power.
