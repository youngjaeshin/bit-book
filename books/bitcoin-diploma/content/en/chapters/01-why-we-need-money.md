# Why We Need Money

## Chapter Digest

Money is introduced as one of humanity’s most powerful tools for freedom because it lets people store effort, compare alternatives, and exchange value across time. The discussion does not start with a narrow definition. It begins with the larger questions that sit underneath money: why people work for it, why different forms of money exist, why some people hold more of it than others, and why money cannot simply be created without cost. Understanding money therefore means understanding scarcity, choice, and the trade-offs that shape human life.

Time preference and opportunity cost show why money matters to the future as much as to the present. If people want to delay consumption, save resources, and choose between immediate satisfaction and later opportunity, they need a medium that can carry value through time. Money is not just a modern convenience. It is a practical tool for planning, prioritizing, and living under conditions where resources are limited.
