# Chapter 3: Time

## Chapter Digest

Human action unfolds across time, and because time is scarce, it must be economized like any other resource. The chapter treats time as the ultimate scarce factor behind opportunity cost, production, planning, and all economic choice. Material abundance, technological progress, and productivity gains matter because they allow human beings to accomplish more within limited lifetimes.

This leads to the concept of time preference: present satisfaction is generally valued more highly than future satisfaction, and this ordering shapes saving, production, and investment decisions. The chapter therefore links economic development to the ability to economize time better, to extend planning horizons, and to redirect action away from immediate consumption toward more productive future-oriented arrangements.

## Key Takeaways

- Time is the ultimate scarce resource behind all economic action.
- Opportunity cost arises because choosing one use of time excludes others.
- Material abundance matters because it frees time and expands action.
- Time preference shapes saving and future-oriented behavior.
- Economic progress is tied to better economizing of time.

## Notable Terms / Claims

- Time scarcity is more fundamental than many material scarcities.
- Opportunity cost is inseparable from action in time.
- Development depends on longer planning horizons and lower time preference.
