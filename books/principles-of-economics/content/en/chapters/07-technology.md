# Chapter 7: Technology

## Chapter Digest

Technology is treated as the product of human reason applied to production. Before production changes in the external world, it is first changed in the mind through ideas about better methods, better combinations, and more effective use of resources. Technological progress is therefore inseparable from freedom, property, and the incentives that allow individuals to test and implement new ideas.

The chapter rejects the notion that technological advance floats free of economic institutions. Innovation flourishes where individuals can accumulate capital, compare costs, and profit from discovering more productive methods. Technology is thus not an autonomous external force acting on the economy; it is one of the main ways human action transforms economic reality.

## Key Takeaways

- Technology is applied reason in production.
- Innovation begins in human ideas before it appears in material output.
- Economic freedom and property rights support technological progress.
- Capital and calculation are necessary for sustained innovation.
- Technology is an endogenous part of economic action, not an external add-on.

## Notable Terms / Claims

- Laissez-faire is linked to technological advance.
- Innovation depends on incentives and institutional freedom.
- Technology expresses purposeful human action.
