# Digital Money

## Chapter Digest

Digital form alone does not make money sound. The central question is whether scarcity, settlement, and ownership remain dependent on centralized trust. Many digital payment systems preserve the trust assumptions of fiat even when the interface changes.

Bitcoin matters because it attempts to bring hard-money logic into digital form. Fixed supply and decentralized verification are what separate it from ordinary electronic payments and from state-issued digital systems. Digital money becomes historically important only when it changes the architecture of monetary trust.

## Key Takeaways

- Digitality alone is not enough to make money sound.
- The key issue is whether centralized trust bottlenecks remain.
- Bitcoin translates hard-money logic into digital form.
- Fixed supply and decentralized verification distinguish Bitcoin.
- Digital money matters when it changes monetary trust itself.
