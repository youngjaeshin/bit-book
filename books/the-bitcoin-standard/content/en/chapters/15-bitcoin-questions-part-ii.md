# Bitcoin Questions II

## Chapter Digest

The second half turns to criminality, attack surfaces, altcoins, and blockchain hype. Open systems are not criminal systems simply because criminals can use them, and vulnerability is not the same thing as easy capture. Bitcoin is not invulnerable, but neither is it uniquely discredited by the misuse of open money.

The unit also distinguishes Bitcoin from generic blockchain enthusiasm. Bitcoin matters because it solves a specific monetary problem: digital scarcity without centralized trust. Many blockchain proposals inherit the cost of decentralization without the monetary necessity that justifies it. Bitcoin stands out because its architecture matches its use case.

## Key Takeaways

- Open monetary systems are not defined by criminal use.
- Attack surfaces exist without implying easy capture.
- Bitcoin is distinct from generic blockchain hype.
- Decentralization is costly and must be justified by a real use case.
- Bitcoin’s structure fits its monetary purpose.
