# Combining, Splitting, and Privacy

## Chapter Digest

Combining and splitting value clarifies how Bitcoin handles value at the transaction level rather than at the account-balance level. Inputs and outputs can be grouped, divided, and recombined, making the system flexible without abandoning traceability. Privacy then enters as a question of how public records and pseudonymous addresses interact. Bitcoin does not offer perfect anonymity, but it creates room for privacy through address practices and limits on direct identity linkage.

The commentary uses these topics to show that form and privacy are inseparable. The way value is structured in transactions affects what can be inferred about ownership and behavior. Privacy is therefore neither automatic nor irrelevant; it is a design and usage question built into the structure of the system.
