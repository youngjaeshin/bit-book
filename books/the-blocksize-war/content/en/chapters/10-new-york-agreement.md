# Chapter 18

## Chapter Digest

The New York Agreement represented the strongest elite attempt to settle the war from above. Major businesses and miners tried to construct a package deal: activate SegWit, then follow with a 2MB hard fork. To its supporters this looked like pragmatic compromise. To its opponents it looked like constitutional bypass — a boardroom settlement of something that only the wider network could legitimate.

What made the NYA so important was not only its content but its form. It embodied the thesis that Bitcoin could be steered by a coalition of large firms and hashpower. The furious backlash it triggered showed that this thesis was itself on trial. Once again, process proved inseparable from substance.

The chapter stands as the climax of elite coordination in the war. It also set the stage for the final showdown over whether social legitimacy or executive dealmaking would define Bitcoin.

## Key Takeaways

- The NYA was the most ambitious top-down settlement attempt.
- It linked SegWit activation to a later hard-fork commitment.
- Opponents saw the agreement as a constitutional bypass.
- The legitimacy of elite coordination became the real issue.
- Process and substance could no longer be separated.
