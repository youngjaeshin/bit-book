Gavin’s Craig Wright announcement lands like a second legitimacy bomb just as the blocksize war is already exhausting everyone. The claim that Craig Steven Wright is Satoshi Nakamoto does not settle anything; it pulls the community into another round of proof, doubt, and personality politics, with Roger Ver and others eager to read Craig as a usable ally. Instead of clarifying Bitcoin’s origin story, the episode shows how brittle hero worship becomes when identity can be staged, amplified, or turned into a weapon in an existing fight.

The DAO hack then gives Bitcoiners a live demonstration of the kind of governance they fear. Ethereum’s response is a contentious hardfork that rewrites history under pressure, and the split into Ethereum and Ethereum Classic becomes a case study in what happens when emergency intervention is treated as a normal tool. That episode sharpens the contrast with Bitcoin: even if the details are not identical, the social lesson is clear. Systems that can be rewritten by a visible inner circle are vulnerable to the same legitimacy crisis that the blocksize war is already exposing.
