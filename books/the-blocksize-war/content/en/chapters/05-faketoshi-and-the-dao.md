# Chapter 9 + Chapter 10

## Chapter Digest

This unit shows the blocksize war colliding with a broader crisis of legitimacy in the crypto space. Craig Wright’s reappearance as “Satoshi” and the Ethereum DAO crisis both shaped Bitcoin discourse by showing how identity claims, emergency governance, and public confusion could destabilize open systems. These episodes were not side dramas; they affected how actors inside Bitcoin assessed credibility, coordination, and the risks of intervention.

The DAO crisis in particular sharpened a contrast. Ethereum’s willingness to rewrite history under pressure became a warning sign for many Bitcoiners who already feared discretionary protocol governance. Wright’s theatrics, meanwhile, demonstrated how charisma, confusion, and media amplification could distort technical culture. Together these episodes made the small-block emphasis on rule robustness look more compelling.

The broader lesson is that governance norms are formed not just by internal argument, but by external cautionary examples. Bitcoin’s constitutional instinct hardened by watching what happened elsewhere.

## Key Takeaways

- Outside crypto events influenced how Bitcoiners interpreted their own governance conflict.
- The DAO crisis highlighted the risks of discretionary intervention.
- Craig Wright’s claims exposed how identity confusion can destabilize discourse.
- Small-block arguments gained force from observing failures elsewhere.
- Governance culture is shaped by cautionary comparison.
