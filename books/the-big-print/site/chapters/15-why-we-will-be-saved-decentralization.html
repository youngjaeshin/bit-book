WHY WE WILL BE SAVED: DECENTRALIZATION

Chapter Digest

Decentralization is presented as the long-term technological and social trend that can counter the failures of centralized systems. Modern history produced concentration because scale, industry, and state power favored it. But newer technologies reverse some of those advantages by allowing smaller actors, distributed networks, and local autonomy to compete again.

Bitcoin belongs to this broader movement. It is not an isolated invention but one expression of a decentralizing arc that also appears in communication, production, and organization. The chapter’s optimism rests on the claim that centralization is not destiny; technology is steadily rebuilding the conditions for self-sovereignty.

Key Takeaways

  • Centralization dominated earlier phases of modern development.
  • New technologies are restoring the viability of decentralization.
  • Bitcoin is part of a wider decentralizing shift.
  • Scale is no longer an automatic argument for concentration.
  • The chapter’s optimism is grounded in technological reversal of centralized power.

Notable Terms / Claims

  • Decentralization is treated as a civilizational trend.
  • Self-reliance and self-sovereignty re-emerge through technology.
  • Bitcoin is one instance of a much broader structural change.
