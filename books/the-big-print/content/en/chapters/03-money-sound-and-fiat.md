# MONEY: SOUND AND FIAT

## Chapter Digest

Money emerges here as a solution to the limits of barter and as a form of social information. A functioning monetary good must allow exchange, accounting, and value storage across time. The chapter moves from this basic definition toward a distinction between monetary forms that preserve integrity and those that can be manipulated by political authority.

Sound money disciplines both individuals and institutions because it preserves purchasing power, rewards saving, and limits arbitrary interference. Fiat money does the opposite: it depends on decree, expands at political discretion, and severs money from a stable anchor. The contrast is therefore not merely technical. It shapes freedom, politics, and the long-run moral character of economic life.

## Key Takeaways

- Money solves the coordination problems of barter and serves as social information.
- A monetary good must function as medium of exchange, unit of account, and store of value.
- Sound money preserves discipline by protecting purchasing power and rewarding saving.
- Fiat money expands by political discretion and weakens monetary integrity.
- The choice between sound and fiat money affects liberty as well as economics.

## Notable Terms / Claims

- Money is treated as information as much as a transactional tool.
- Sound money and fiat money are contrasted in moral and political, not only technical, terms.
- Monetary design influences the structure of freedom and state power.
