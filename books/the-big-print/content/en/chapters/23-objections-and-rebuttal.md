# OBJECTIONS AND REBUTTAL

## Chapter Digest

Common objections to sound money and Bitcoin are gathered here in order to show that the standard case against them is weaker than it appears. Critics argue that gold standards caused depression, deflation always destroys growth, or Bitcoin is unfair, impractical, or socially harmful. These claims are answered by reframing the historical record and by distinguishing genuine monetary discipline from crises caused by leverage, intervention, and prior distortion.

The chapter’s method is defensive but also clarifying. Each objection reveals what the book takes to be the core divide: whether monetary stability and limits are themselves the problem, or whether the real problem is the debt-heavy fiat system that constantly blames its own consequences on discipline.

## Key Takeaways

- Standard objections to sound money and Bitcoin are taken up directly.
- Historical and conceptual confusion are central to many criticisms.
- Deflation and monetary discipline are defended against common attacks.
- The chapter distinguishes sound-money constraints from fiat-generated crises.
- Rebuttal is used to sharpen the book’s core divide.

## Notable Terms / Claims

- Many objections are treated as reversed causality.
- Sound money is defended by re-reading history rather than ignoring it.
- Criticism of Bitcoin often inherits criticism of monetary restraint more broadly.
