# CALL TO ACTION

## Chapter Digest

The coming period is described as financially and politically turbulent, which turns the book’s thesis into a practical demand for preparation. Waiting passively is not presented as prudence. Individuals must protect themselves, strengthen their monetary understanding, and position themselves before the disorder intensifies.

The chapter therefore shifts from diagnosis and theory to responsibility. Preparation begins at the personal level but does not end there. Those who understand the system are expected to act, educate, and help others navigate a period that is likely to become more chaotic rather than less.

## Key Takeaways

- Turbulence over the next decade is treated as highly likely.
- Preparation is framed as a practical obligation.
- Personal action comes before broader social action.
- Monetary understanding is part of self-protection.
- Knowledge creates responsibility to help others.

## Notable Terms / Claims

- The chapter functions as a bridge from theory to action.
- Self-protection is not treated as selfishness but as prerequisite for helping others.
- The call to action is rooted in expected disorder ahead.
