# SKEPTICISM

## Chapter Digest

Skepticism toward Bitcoin is treated as understandable but often misdirected. Much of it comes from lumping Bitcoin together with the wider world of cryptocurrencies, scams, and speculative noise. The chapter insists that Bitcoin must be judged on its own properties rather than by association with the surrounding industry.

Technological skepticism is also addressed as part of the adoption curve of every major breakthrough. New monetary technology appears strange before it appears obvious. The point is not that every objection is foolish, but that many objections fail because they target caricatures rather than Bitcoin itself.

## Key Takeaways

- Skepticism is acknowledged but often traced to category confusion.
- Bitcoin is sharply distinguished from other cryptocurrencies.
- Technological unfamiliarity is part of normal adoption.
- Many objections target caricatures rather than the protocol’s core properties.
- The chapter reframes skepticism as a stage to be worked through, not a final verdict.

## Notable Terms / Claims

- “Bitcoin is not crypto” is a key distinction.
- Adoption is treated as cumulative despite resistance.
- Misconception is one of the main barriers to understanding Bitcoin.
