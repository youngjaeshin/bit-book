# POLICY RESPONSES

## Chapter Digest

A monetary critique is not left at complaint alone; the chapter attempts to sketch an alternative path. Policy reform is imagined in terms of monetary reset, institutional restraint, and a direct challenge to the dominance of the Federal Reserve and Keynesian policy assumptions. The objective is to restore discipline rather than add more layers of management.

At the same time, the chapter recognizes how difficult such reform would be within the existing political order. The mainstream response continues to favor more intervention, more debt accommodation, and more centralized control. Policy choice is therefore framed as a fork between restoring limits and doubling down on fiat management.

## Key Takeaways

- The chapter moves from criticism to an alternative policy framework.
- Monetary reset and institutional restraint are central themes.
- Ending or curbing the Fed is treated as a serious reform direction.
- Mainstream policy is expected to favor more intervention instead.
- The policy choice is cast as limits versus deeper fiat management.

## Notable Terms / Claims

- Monetary reset is treated as a policy project as well as a market outcome.
- Institutional restraint matters more than technocratic fine-tuning.
- Mainstream reform remains trapped inside the same fiat assumptions.
