# SOUND MONEY IS A MORAL ISSUE

## Chapter Digest

Monetary corruption is presented here as an old human temptation: to gain something for nothing by debasing measures, diluting money, or exploiting trust. The chapter places modern monetary debasement in a long historical line that includes counterfeiting, cheating in trade, and political abuse of standards. The issue is not merely efficiency. It is honesty.

Sound money is therefore framed as a moral requirement because fair exchange depends on stable measures. Once money itself is distorted, the whole field of economic relations becomes less truthful. Monetary debasement does not simply alter prices; it weakens justice in trade, rewards deception, and makes theft easier to hide behind policy language.

## Key Takeaways

- Monetary manipulation is presented as a recurring moral failure, not just a technical policy error.
- Historical debasement and modern inflation belong to the same pattern.
- Fair exchange requires honest and stable measures.
- Sound money is linked to justice, integrity, and social trust.
- Monetary debasement hides forms of theft behind institutional legitimacy.

## Notable Terms / Claims

- Sound money is treated as a moral issue before it is an economic preference.
- Debasement is linked to cheating on weights and measures.
- Inflation is framed as disguised injustice in exchange.
