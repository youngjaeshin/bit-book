# 28 Home Mining Basics

## Chapter Digest

Home mining is framed as a way to understand Bitcoin’s security from the hardware side. It explains mining basics, distinguishes mining methods, walks through profitability, pool reward systems, Stratum, pool limits, Datum, and the meaning of mining sovereignty so the reader can see why a miner is closer to an infrastructure operator than a casual user.

The practical builds with Bitaxe Gamma 601 and Avalon Nano 3 make that theory concrete. The reader sees how to assemble the hardware, connect power and network, update firmware, and switch between solo mining and pool participation. The option to receive rewards through Lightning keeps the process tied to the broader spending and self-custody stack already built elsewhere in the book.

Its deeper point is that mining is not only about profit. It is about touching the physical cost that underwrites Bitcoin and understanding why the network remains anchored to real-world energy and operations.
