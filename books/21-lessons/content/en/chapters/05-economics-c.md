# Economics C

## Chapter Digest

Fractional reserve banking and quantitative easing are presented as euphemisms that hide the absurdity of lending money that does not exist and socializing the damage when the structure fails. The prose keeps insisting that the system is more theatrical than it first appears: jargon makes ordinary people overlook how much trust and leverage are being manufactured out of thin air.

The contrast with sound money is the moral center of the section. Soft money invites discretion, favoritism, and repeated rescue, while sound money constrains power by constraining issuance. Bitcoin matters here because it removes the need to trust human restraint and replaces it with rules enforced by mathematics and distributed verification.

By the end of the economic section, Bitcoin is no longer just an asset or payment rail. It becomes the standard against which the legitimacy of the existing monetary order is measured, and the old system looks increasingly like a fragile arrangement of credit, rescue, and ritualized intervention.
