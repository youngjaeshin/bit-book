# Technology B

## Chapter Digest

The second technology movement shifts from security to civilizational design. Moving slowly is a virtue because systems that secure value cannot be treated like consumer apps. Stability, auditability, and careful iteration matter more than novelty, so the book treats slow change as a protection for neutrality rather than as a flaw.

Privacy is rehabilitated in the same way. It is not a suspicious preference but a condition of freedom, dignity, and open society. Gigi aligns Bitcoin with the cypherpunk tradition by insisting that code, not pleading, is what actually changes the boundaries of political possibility.

The closing metaphors make the point from another angle. Bitcoin behaves like a protocol, an organism, and a social layer at once, so no single image can contain it. Digital gold, the internet of money, a timechain, and a truth machine each illuminate something useful, but none exhaust the system. Bitcoin’s technology and values are fused, and future institutions will have to build around that fact.
