# Economics B

## Chapter Digest

Money is treated as a civilizational coordination tool before it is treated as a government token. Seashells, metals, paper claims, and bitcoin all occupy the monetary role because people use them to escape the inefficiency of barter. Once money is understood as half of every transaction, control over money creation appears as an extraordinary form of power.

That is why monetary history becomes a history of debasement. Commodity money and representative money once anchored value to something costly and difficult to fake, but fiat money breaks that anchor by making state decree the decisive fact. Fiat is not valuable because of intrinsic qualities; it is valuable because political force compels acceptance.

Bitcoin enters this story as a re-anchoring rather than a novelty detached from the past. The historical arc revisits shells, gold, silver, coin clipping, paper certificates, and legal tender to show that the real question is whether money emerges from the market or is imposed from above. Monetary history is the story of how societies either preserve or lose honest price signals.
