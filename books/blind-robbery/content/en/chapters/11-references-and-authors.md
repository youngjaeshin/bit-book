# References & About the Authors

## Chapter Digest

The references and author notes place the argument inside a larger intellectual lineage rather than in isolation. The bibliography reaches back through Mises, Hayek, Rothbard, Baader, Hülsmann, Hoppe, and other critics of interventionism and monetary distortion, making clear that the book is drawing on an older tradition of political economy rather than improvising a new complaint. That matters because the central claims about money, inflation, and state power are not presented as slogans; they are part of a sustained body of thought about how societies get rich, how they get impoverished, and how rulers hide the difference.

The author notes serve a similar purpose. They place the voices behind the text in relation to that tradition and remind the reader that the book is not just warning about bad policy but participating in a longstanding effort to recover honest language about money. That closing note reinforces a broader message: good money, sound institutions, and intellectual clarity belong together, and the references are part of the evidence. Following those sources lets readers trace each claim back to the tradition that produced it.
