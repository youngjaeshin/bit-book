# Why Everyone Wants Dollars

## Chapter Digest

People often want dollars not because the dollar is healthy money, but because the whole system is organized around it. Global debt, reserve practices, trade settlement, and access to modern economic networks all reinforce demand for the currency. That creates a contradiction: people reach for dollars because they must operate inside a dollar-based world, even as the dollar regime weakens their purchasing power over time.

This gives the incumbent system enormous staying power. A currency can be weak as a long-term store of value and still remain unavoidable because contracts, liabilities, and institutions depend on it. Wanting a currency is not the same as believing it is sound, and that distinction matters when considering how difficult it is to move away from broken money.
