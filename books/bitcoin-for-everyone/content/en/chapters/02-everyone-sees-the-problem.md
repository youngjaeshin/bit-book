# Everyone Feels the Problem

## Chapter Digest

Prices keep rising while wages fail to keep pace, so the cost of housing, food, healthcare, education, and ordinary life steadily becomes harder to absorb. That deterioration is often treated as normal, but the chapter argues that normalizing inflation is part of the damage. The promise that hard work and restraint will produce stability grows less believable each year.

The deeper diagnosis is monetary. Broken money shifts purchasing power upward, makes social mobility harder, and hides the real scale of the loss behind official measures that often understate how much daily life has become more expensive. Inflation is not just higher prices; it is a system that turns effort into stagnation and then asks people to blame themselves.
