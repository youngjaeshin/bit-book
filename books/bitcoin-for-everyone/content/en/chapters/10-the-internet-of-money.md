# The Internet of Money

## Chapter Digest

Bitcoin is compared to the internet because both change the architecture of exchange. The internet let information move across the world without centralized gatekeepers, and Bitcoin lets value move across a global network without needing banks, states, or legacy payment rails to grant permission. The point is not simply to add another financial product but to change how transfer itself works.

Network effects matter here. As more people join, the system becomes more useful and more resilient. Bitcoin’s importance therefore cannot be measured only by current usage. Like the internet in its early years, its larger significance appears as the network grows and direct, permissionless exchange becomes more normal.
