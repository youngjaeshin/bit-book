# Why Everyone Needs Money

## Chapter Digest

Money is not a convenience layered on top of life. It is the social technology that organizes trade, calculation, and saving. Here the focus is on the store-of-value function because that is where modern currency fails most visibly: money can still clear a payment today and still be unfit for carrying effort safely into the future.

Hard money encourages patience, thrift, and longer planning horizons because saved purchasing power survives. Weak money does the opposite, pushing people toward immediacy, speculation, and the hunt for anything that can outrun debasement. Monetary quality therefore shapes culture as well as finance, influencing whether a society rewards durability, family stability, and long-term commitment or favors extraction and disposability.
