# What School Should Have Taught Us

## Chapter Digest

The root problem is an expanding money supply that keeps diluting the value of each existing unit. Looking at measures such as M2 makes it clear that monetary expansion is not a one-time emergency response but a long-running structural process. Once that is understood, inflation stops looking accidental and starts looking built into the system.

That dilution changes the meaning of work, saving, and ownership. Nominal wage gains can hide a loss in real purchasing power, and scarce assets such as housing become increasingly unreachable for younger households. Money creation also reshapes behavior beyond the household: it pushes people and firms toward short-term thinking, rewards quick speculation over patience, and alters the time horizon of society itself.
