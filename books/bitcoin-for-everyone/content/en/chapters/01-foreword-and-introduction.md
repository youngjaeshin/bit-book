# Foreword & Introduction

## Chapter Digest

Money is so woven into daily life that people often stop asking what it is until the system begins failing them. The personal struggle to keep up is not just a matter of discipline or luck; it points to a monetary order that quietly drains purchasing power while teaching people to blame themselves for falling behind. Families, workers, savers, and even successful professionals all end up navigating the same unstable ground.

Bitcoin is presented as a way to see that hidden structure clearly. Its significance is not limited to price speculation. It exposes how wealth concentration, political frustration, and permanent financial anxiety are tied to the design of money itself, and it suggests that the modern economy has normalized debt, dependence, and insecurity as if they were unavoidable.

The narrative becomes concrete through family history and financial collapse. Immigration, hard work, and faith in the American Dream all meet a system that can still fail honest people. What looked like personal inadequacy turns out to be life inside a broken monetary arrangement, and Bitcoin matters because it points to a different one.
