# Federal Reserve System

## Chapter Digest

The Federal Reserve is the American expression of the same logic. It sits over deposits, reserves, and interbank settlement, shaping the conditions under which lower layers can expand or fail. Understanding dollars requires more than watching notes and bank balances; it requires seeing the institution that conditions them all.

Early American money was a mixed and unstable arrangement, and the Fed emerged as the layer that would coordinate reserves and crisis response. It is not a passive referee. It changes the environment on which banks, markets, and the dollar itself operate, making the U.S. monetary system legible as a managed hierarchy rather than a flat currency.
