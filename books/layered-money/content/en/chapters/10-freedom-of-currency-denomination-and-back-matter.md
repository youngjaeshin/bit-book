# Freedom of Currency Denomination & Back Matter

## Chapter Digest

Bitcoin makes denomination itself a matter of choice. People can begin to hold, price, save, and contract in the unit that best fits their needs rather than inheriting a state currency by default. That shift is framed as monetary emancipation, because the unit of account shapes how reality is measured and who gets to define it.

The closing material extends that claim into a wider future of money. BTC can serve as a neutral anchor while banks, stablecoins, and state digital money continue to exist for different purposes in a multipolar system. The acknowledgements and references place the argument inside a larger network of thinkers, builders, and institutions, showing that the future of money is not a single endpoint but a field of choice.

The final pages therefore close by widening the map rather than narrowing it. Monetary freedom is not presented as an isolated feature of Bitcoin; it is the condition that makes layered money legible, contestable, and open to human choice.
