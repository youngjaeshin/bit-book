# Dollar in Disrepair

## Chapter Digest

The dollar remains the system’s reference point even as its structure wears down. It sits inside a web of bookkeepers’ entries, reference rates, money market funds, and interbank promises that only function when trust is intact. Once trust starts to fail, the whole layered arrangement becomes more fragile than its size suggests.

Disrepair is not collapse but erosion. The dollar survives because it is deeply embedded in contracts and institutions, yet that same centrality means that every weakness spreads widely. When the central layer becomes the lender of only resort, the system reveals how much of its apparent strength depends on continual intervention rather than on durable monetary soundness.
