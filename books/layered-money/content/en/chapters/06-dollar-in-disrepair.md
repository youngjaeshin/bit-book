# Dollar in Disrepair

## Chapter Digest

The dollar remains dominant while increasingly fragile. Its role as reserve currency, settlement medium, and global denominator gives it unusual resilience, but this same centrality also magnifies the consequences of its deterioration. The chapter shows a system that is still indispensable but no longer sound.

Disrepair does not mean immediate disappearance. It means the structure continues while its internal weaknesses deepen. The dollar’s monetary status survives through network position and institutional dependence even as trust in its long-run integrity weakens. This makes dollar decline a process of erosion more than a single event.

## Key Takeaways

- The dollar can remain dominant while becoming less sound.
- Centrality increases both resilience and systemic fragility.
- Disrepair is an erosive process rather than instant collapse.
- Institutional dependence sustains the dollar beyond strict monetary merit.
- Reserve status does not guarantee integrity.
